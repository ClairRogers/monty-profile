Basics

Monty is what the kids call Chaotic Stupid. He's small, wildly energetic, younger than most, and therefore everyone's self-assigned Annoying Little Brother. He may not be the smartest or the strongest, but he's quick and tireless, and genuinely kind at heart.

Body Type

Monty is rather small, even for his age, and quite lanky. Being the runt of his litter, he will probably never be quite as big as the other cats. He’s also still growing into his body, so he has long limbs and paws that are too big for him at his size. His tail is very expressive and almost always up, with a jaunty kink at the tip. His left canine is a snaggletooth.

Fur Texture

Monty’s fur is mostly of the medium- to short-length variety. It would be soft and fluffy (almost like kit fur) if he cleaned it… but he doesn’t. He almost completely ignores common grooming standards, and his fur tends to be dirty and stick up in random directions.

Color

Monty is basically a red marbled tabby. Most of his fur is reddish orange with dark swooping stripes, and a more tan/cream undercoat. His toes and tail tip are white, as well as a spot on his forehead and a little v-shape on his chest that sometimes looks like a heart. His eyes are blue.

Personality

Monty is quite the annoying little guy, in truth. Frankly, he is probably no one’s favorite cat, but everyone somehow just tolerates his existence anyway. He is ADHD with an emphasis on the “hyperactive” part. He is constantly in motion and constantly has a high amount of energy and if you’re in his presence he probably won’t leave you alone. He likes to brag a lot but never has anything awesome to brag about, so really he just tells a bunch of boring and/or stupid stories that no one else is impressed by. But he doesn’t mind if no one really cares, as long as they listen with some fraction of attention.

He’s a severe kleptomaniac and will steal things from you. He’s not specific. He’ll steal your food, your sleeping spot, your accessoreis, shiny rocks… anything he finds interesting at the time. He’s absolutely unapologetic about it too, but if you ask, he’ll happily give it back. Basically if you find yourself missing something, Monty probably stole it.

There are a great many things Monty loves, including shiny objects, chasing leaves, hanging out with the Cool Older Kids, and pranking others. Rush is quite the trickster--as well as just stealing everyone’s stuff all the time, he also just loves to trick others and outsmart them for a laugh. He’s pretty shameless about it but also very childish, oftentimes failing to realize when he's gone too far. It’s not one of his better traits.

Monty is a veritable wellspring of obscure and mostly useless information, especially when it comes to the Legendaries. He's a huge fanboy. He wouldn’t be able to tell you how to catch a mouse, but he would be able to tell you the name, family, and life story of almost any Legendary cat. He is intelligent, but he just doesn’t have time for the nonsense all the older cats want to teach him about hunting and surviving and being a productive member of society (blah blah blah). He cares about other cats, and will thus absorb and store any information about them he thinks is interesting.

Despite the fact that oftentimes others find him annoying, he likes being around a lot of other cats. Especially the older boys--he's a huge groupie, and looks up to almost anyone older than him. He thinks they're SO cool and wants to be just like them, which is why he's determined to prove himself in these trials. He often doesn’t do well alone, and prefers the company of others. He lives a carefree and happy life, and he lives in the moment and takes things as they come and doesn’t let stuff drag him down. He's a great guy to be around if you need cheered up, because he will certainly make every effort to do so, even if he doesn't know or understand what you're sad about. He means well, and has a good heart. If you get to know him in this way, he is loveable, even if he’s too much to handle for extended periods of time.

History

Short Version

  • Monty was born in Plains Colony to his mother, Wynona, and an unknown father.
  • He was one of a litter of five--while all of his siblings were strong and healthy, he was the runt of the litter… noticeably smaller and weaker.
  • Wynona, thinking his chances of survival were slim, essentially abandoned him to focus her attending on keeping her other four kits alive.
  • He was taken in by Quinn, a childhood friend of Wynona who couldn’t stand by and watch the little guy die. He even gave him his name, since Wynona hadn’t wanted to name him for fear of him dying.
  • Still very young, Monty was taken with Quinn when he and his mate, Desmond, deserted the Plains Corps (and Plains Colony itself) after a bad run-in that left Desmond injured.
  • The trio escaped Plains Colony and headed for Forest Colony, Quinn taking care both of young Monty and injured Desmond.
  • After a couple weeks on their own, they eventually came to Forest Colony. There, they were welcomed in by the locals and taken care of.
  • They spend several months at Forest Colony while Monty grew up and Desmond recovered from his injuries. Monty began showing his penchant for being extremely mischievous and getting into all kinds of trouble. Quinn especially is always very patient and understanding with him.
  • When hearing of the call to Paradise Valley, Monty was wildly excited and begged his dads to let him go.
  • Quinn doesn’t want to split up, but Desmond insists. Monty is RARING to go anyway. Desmond basically tells Quinn to go drop him off, and then come back to find me.
  • Quinn and Monty set off for Paradise Valley. It was going to take them a week, if not longer, to get there, but Monty is quite determined.
  • Several days in Quinn wakes up one morning with a fierce migraine. He thinks nothing of it, but it persists all day, getting worse and worse.
  • The second day, Quinn does something he has never done before: he loses his patience and snaps at Monty. Both Monty and Quinn are taken aback. It’s then that Quinn realizes what’s happening to him.
  • On the third day, Quinn knows he has the red plague and is about to die. Meanwhile, Monty is oblivious to his dad’s symptoms, focused only on the goal of getting to Paradise Valley and becoming Cool™. Quinn, not wanting Monty to have to witness what’s about to happen to him, stops and tells Monty that he should go on without him.
  • Quinn knows he’ll never see Monty again, but Monty is still oblivious. Excited to finally be on his own and away from his parents, he only gives a quick goodbye before dashing off.
  • (Quinn watches him go and waits until long after he can’t see him anymore).
  • Monty relishes his alone time (though nights alone scare him a bit). It only takes a couple more days before he reaches Paradise Valley. Seeing so many older, cooler cats there, he is Very Excited and determined to face the trials and prove himself.
